Part 1: Competitive Analysis

Select at least 3 different websites with a favoriting function:

eBay: Has favoriting functions that allow users to save products they are interested in. The website uses a shopping cart as an aggregator to keep track of selected items.

Tiktok: Has a “Save” feature that allows users to save posts for later viewing. The platform incorporates aggregators through a collection of the saved items.

Simplify Jobs: Has a favoriting or bookmarking option for users to save appealing jobs. The website incorporates aggregators through a “Saved Jobs” section on the home page.

eBay excels in offering expanded dropdown menus, adaptive layout, visual organization, and collaborative filtering. These features contribute to a user-friendly experience by providing easy access to a wide range of options, adapting to different devices, presenting information in an organized manner, and recommending relevant products based on user behavior. TikTok lacks expanded dropdown menus and adaptive layout but compensates with customizable filters (in the form of hashtags), visual organization, and collaborative filtering. While it may not have extensive menu options or a fully responsive design, TikTok allows users to personalize their content preferences, presents videos in an engaging layout, and uses collaborative filtering for tailored recommendations. Simplify Jobs offers expanded dropdown menus, adaptive layout, and collaborative filtering but lacks customizable filters and good visual organization. While it provides options in a structured manner, personalized recommendations, and adjusts to different screen sizes, the absence of customizable filters and visual cues for organization may impact user engagement and satisfaction with the feature.

When implementing a list-based interface, I would focus on creating a user-friendly and visually appealing design that enhances usability and engagement. The features and designs I would like to implement based on the successful factors identified and the improvements needed are an adaptive layout, customizable filters (keyword search), visual organization, simplistic and intuitive navigation, and with enough time, collaborative filtering.

Part 3: Functionality

The intention of this app is to help users find researchers that match their interests. The app will have a list of researchers that users can filter and sort based on their research areas. The app will also have an aggregator that will show favorited researchers that match the user's interests.

The app includes a way to filter researcher cards by research area. The user can select a research area from a dropdown menu and the researcher cards will update to show only the researchers that match the selected research area.

The app includes a way to filter researcher cards by keywords in the researcher's description. The user can enter a keyword in the search bar and the researcher cards will update to show only the researchers that have the keyword in their description.

The app includes a way to sort researcher cards by alphabetical order of the research area. They can be filtered in ascending and descending order.

A button at the top of the page allows the user to reset the filters and sorts.

Users can add researcher cards to their list of favorites. The aggregator at the right side of the page will update to show the researchers in the list of favorites. Once added to the list of favorites, each researcher has a corresponding button that allows the user to remove the researcher from the list of favorites.

Filter & sort are compatible and work in combination with each other. The user can filter by research area and then sort the filtered researchers by alphabetical order of the research area. The user can also filter by keyword and search for descriptions that match the keyword.
